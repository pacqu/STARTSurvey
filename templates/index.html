Why Attend College?

By attending college and earning a degree, students gain a higher level of education, become more socially literate, start on a path of social mobility, and (perhaps most importantly) allows for the attainment of a higher standard of living by opening them up to a job market increasing searching for graduates.

Despite these benefits, there are still people who do not continue on into college after high school, just as there are still people who despite having the desire to earn a degree, will not enroll in college. Why? One of these obstacles is the greater costs becoming associated with college.

College Affordability as an Obstacle

Although college is a natural step in the educational journeys for some students, for many it is not a viable choice. Students who wish to attend college may not be able to due to economic circumstances. Some reasons include their inability to afford to pay for college, draw out enough loans, or attain financial aid .

The Excelsior Scholarship

Governor Cuomo is taking a step to close the gap of low-income students who are unable to pay for college by creating the Excelsior Scholarship. With this scholarship, families and individuals making up to $125,000 a year can attend a public college in New York State tuition-free . However, to maximize the benefits of this, it is important to spread awareness of the scholarship to students who qualify.
